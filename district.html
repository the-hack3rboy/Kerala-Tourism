Top Attractions

Pattanamthitta

Pathanamthitta is a beautiful district located in the southern Indian state of Kerala. It is known for its lush green landscapes, serene backwaters, and rich cultural heritage. The district is blessed with several rivers, hills, and forests, which make it a popular destination for nature lovers and adventure enthusiasts. Pathanamthitta is also home to several pilgrimage centers, including the famous Sabarimala temple, which attracts millions of devotees every year. The district is also known for its traditional art forms, such as Kathakali, Mohiniyattam, and Theyyam, which are performed in various temples and cultural events throughout the year. In addition to its natural beauty and cultural heritage, Pathanamthitta is also known for its delicious cuisine, which includes a variety of vegetarian and non-vegetarian dishes. The district is particularly famous for its traditional Kerala-style meals, which are served on banana leaves and include a variety of curries, rice, and other side dishes. Overall, Pathanamthitta is a must-visit destination for anyone looking to experience the rich culture and natural beauty of Kerala. With its stunning landscapes, vibrant culture, and mouth-watering cuisine, the district is sure to leave a lasting impression on anyone who visits.

Alappuzha

Alappuzha, also known as Alleppey, is a coastal city located in the southern Indian state of Kerala. Famous for its intricate network of backwaters, lagoons, and canals, Alappuzha is often referred to as the "Venice of the East". The city is a major hub for houseboat tourism, where visitors can rent traditional wooden boats to cruise the serene waterways, surrounded by lush greenery and charming villages. Alappuzha is also known for its beautiful beaches, including Alappuzha Beach and Marari Beach, which attract visitors from all over the world. The city is steeped in history and culture, with numerous ancient temples and churches, as well as a thriving arts and crafts scene. Alappuzha is also famous for its annual Nehru Trophy Boat Race, a colorful event that attracts thousands of spectators to watch the traditional snake boat races on the Punnamada Lake.

Kozhikode

Kozhikode, also known as Calicut, is a coastal city located in the Indian state of Kerala. It was historically an important trading hub, known for its thriving spice trade with foreign countries, particularly with Arab traders. Today, Kozhikode is a bustling city that retains much of its cultural and historical charm. It is home to several historical landmarks, including the Kozhikode Beach, the Mananchira Square, and the Kozhikode Backwaters, which attract visitors from all over the world. The city is also known for its mouth-watering cuisine, particularly its delectable seafood dishes that are famous throughout the state. Kozhikode is also home to several prestigious educational institutions and is known for its contributions to the field of education. Overall, Kozhikode is a vibrant and dynamic city that seamlessly blends its rich history with modernity, making it a must-visit destination for tourists and locals alike.

Palakkad

Palakkad, also known as Palghat, is a city located in the Indian state of Kerala. It is situated at the foothills of the Western Ghats and is known for its scenic beauty and rich cultural heritage. Palakkad is famous for its lush green forests, cascading waterfalls, and picturesque hills, making it a popular destination for nature enthusiasts. The city is also known for its historical significance, with several ancient forts, temples, and monuments that attract tourists from all over the country. One of the most famous attractions in Palakkad is the Palakkad Fort, a well-preserved 18th-century fort that showcases the city's rich history. Palakkad is also known for its traditional art and culture, including the famous folk dance form of Kerala, Theyyam, and the unique martial art form of Kalaripayattu. The city is also famous for its mouth-watering cuisine, including the famous Palakkad sambar, which is a staple in many South Indian households. Overall, Palakkad is a city that offers a perfect blend of nature, history, and culture, making it a must-visit destination for travelers.

Thiruvanthapuram

Thiruvananthapuram, also known as Trivandrum, is the capital city of the Indian state of Kerala. Located on the southwestern coast of India, the city is known for its rich cultural heritage, beautiful beaches, and historic landmarks. The city is home to several famous temples, including the Sree Padmanabhaswamy Temple, which is one of the wealthiest temples in India and is renowned for its intricate architecture and intricate carvings. Thiruvananthapuram is also famous for its stunning beaches, such as Kovalam Beach and Varkala Beach, which attract visitors from all over the world. The city is also known for its beautiful parks and gardens, such as the Napier Museum and the Kanakakunnu Palace, which are popular tourist attractions. Thiruvananthapuram is also a major hub for education, with several prestigious universities and research institutes located in the city. The city is also known for its delicious cuisine, including spicy seafood dishes, crispy dosas, and sweet desserts. Overall, Thiruvananthapuram is a vibrant and dynamic city that offers a perfect blend of history, culture, and natural beauty, making it a must-visit destination for travelers.

kottayam

Kottayam is a district located in the southern Indian state of Kerala. Known as the "Land of Letters, Lakes, and Latex," Kottayam is famous for its scenic beauty, rich culture, and unique cuisine. The district is situated on the banks of the Meenachil River and is surrounded by the Western Ghats, which lend it a serene and picturesque charm. Kottayam is renowned for its numerous educational institutions, including the Mahatma Gandhi University, which attracts students from across the country. The district is also a major hub for the rubber industry, with vast plantations spread across its verdant landscape. Kottayam is home to several ancient temples, churches, and mosques that reflect the region's rich cultural heritage. The Thirunakkara Mahadeva Temple, Vaikom Mahadeva Temple, and St. Mary's Church are just a few of the notable landmarks in the district. Kottayam's cuisine is a blend of traditional Kerala dishes and unique local delicacies, such as the famous "meen pollichathu" (fish baked in banana leaf) and "karimeen fry" (pearl spot fish fry). The district is also known for its toddy, a local alcoholic beverage made from the sap of coconut palms.
